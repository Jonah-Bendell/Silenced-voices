Artifact One

B.Y.O.B.

Anti-war protest and political critique that exposes the cost of spectacle.

2005

Mezmerize

Anti-war protest and political critique

Grammy Award winner for Best Hard Rock Performance

About This Artifact

B.Y.O.B. is a 2005 protest song that critiques the political forces behind modern warfare. The song gives voice to working-class and marginalized Americans who are disproportionately sent to fight wars they did not choose, while political and corporate leaders remain protected from the consequences. Through sharp satire lyrics and violent sounds, the song exposes the hypocrisy of calling war patriotic when it is just masked suffering for the powerfuls will. In doing so, B.Y.O.B. resists silence by pinpointing the true irony of the modern wars they resist, refusing to allow war to be presented as noble or detached from real lives.

Artifact Two

Cigaro

Satirical critique of toxic masculinity and distorted authority.

2005

Mezmerize

Satirical critique of toxic masculinity

"We're the regulators that deregulate / We're the animators that de-animate"

About This Artifact

We're the regulators that deregulate / We're the animators that de-animate / We're cool, in denial We're the cruel regulators smoking cigaro, cigaro, cigar / Cool, in denial We're the cruel regulators smoking cigaro, cigaro, cigaro

Cigaro is a satirical song that belittles masculinity, dominance, and authority to the point of absurdity. The limited yet powerful lyrics reflect the stupidity of military comparison and the effect of it. The song represents voices marginalized by systems that hold power with aggression, humiliation, and control, exposing how authority often speaks at people rather than listening to them. In doing so, Cigaro resists stereotypes by revealing how domination culture dehumanizes both those who wield power and those silenced by it.

Artifact Four

P.L.U.C.K.

Armenian Genocide recognition and the refusal of erasure.

1998

System of a Down

Armenian Genocide recognition

Politically Lying, Unholy, Cowardly Killers

About This Artifact

Revolution, the only solution / The armed response of an entire nation / Revolution, the only solution / We've taken all their s**t, now it's time for restitution / The plan was mastered and called genocide / You took all the children and then we died / The few that remained were never found / All in a system, down

P.L.U.C.K. is a protest song that directly addresses the Armenian Genocide and the global denial. The song gives voice to descendants of genocide survivors whose history has been politically erased, silenced, or minimized by dominant narratives as they have been themselves being Armenian American. Through aggressive musical structure, the song refuses reconciliation without acknowledgment, insisting that memory itself is an act of resistance. In saving testimonies through sound, P.L.U.C.K. challenges the stereotype that historical violence belongs only to the past.

Artifact Five

Prison Song

Prison privatization and the machinery of mass incarceration.

2001

Toxicity

Prison Privatization

About This Artifact

Prison Song reveals the problems with the American Prison system showing it as a mechanism of silencing and economic gain rather than justice. The song gives voice to communities disproportionately targeted by policing and sentencing, whose lives are controlled, confined, and removed from public empathy. Using rapid lyrics and fast rythym, the song mirrors the mechinary of a industrial prison complex.By naming these structures directly, System of a Down refuses the narrative that mass incarceration is neutral or necessary, insisting instead on recognition of the human cost behind institutional power.
